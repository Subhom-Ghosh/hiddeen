Bengali Gastronomy

A Feast of Flavours

Mustard, hilsa, and the gentle heat of a slow-cooked tradition — explore the full culinary heritage of Bengal.

Macher Jhol

মাছের ঝোল

The quintessential Bengali fish curry — a light, turmeric-bright broth with rohu or katla, tomatoes, and potatoes. Simple, soul-restoring perfection.

Shorshe Ilish

সর্ষে ইলিশ

Hilsa fish — the king of Bengali tables — steamed in a pungent mustard paste with green chilies. A seasonal monsoon delicacy of extraordinary depth.

Kosha Mangsho

কষা মাংস

Slow-braised mutton in a deep, dark gravy of caramelized onions, yogurt, and warming spices. The proud centerpiece of every Bengali celebration.

Chingri Malaikari

চিংড়ি মালাইকারি

Prawns simmered in velvety coconut milk with whole spices — a rich, aromatic curry that speaks of Bengal's coastal abundance.

Aloo Posto

আলু পোস্তো

Potatoes cooked in a paste of white poppy seeds — nutty, creamy and utterly comforting. The beloved everyday dish no Bengali can live without.

Mishti Pulao

মিষ্টি পোলাও

Fragrant sweet rice with ghee, raisins, and cashews — the perfect companion to rich non-vegetarian curries at festivals and weddings.

Luchi-Alur Dom

লুচি আলুর দম

Puffy fried bread paired with spicy slow-cooked potato curry — a Sunday morning ritual and quintessential Bengali comfort food.

Bhapa Ilish

ভাপা ইলিশ

Hilsa steamed in mustard and coconut paste inside banana leaf — silky, pungent, and deeply aromatic. The pinnacle of monsoon Bengali cooking.

Puchka

পুচকা

Crispy semolina shells filled with spiced potato mash and tangy tamarind water — Kolkata's most beloved street food, an evening ritual.

Kathi Roll

কাঠি রোল

Invented at Nizam's in Kolkata — flaky paratha wrapped around kebab, egg, and chutney. The original Indian street wrap.

Jhalmuri

ঝালমুড়ি

Puffed rice tossed with mustard oil, green chilies, onions, and spices in a paper cone — Kolkata's iconic walk-and-eat snack.

Beguni

বেগুনি

Sliced eggplant dipped in spiced gram flour batter and deep-fried to golden perfection — the essential monsoon companion alongside khichuri.

Doi Maach

দই মাছ

Fish simmered in creamy yogurt sauce with mild spices — a rich, comforting dish with a silky tang, perfect for special occasions.

Shukto

শুক্তো

A unique bitter-sweet medley of vegetables in milk and mustard — the traditional Bengali meal opener that cleanses the palate.
