The Sweet Life

Bengali মিস্টি

Chhena molded into masterpieces, steeped in sugar syrup and centuries of devotion — explore every sweet Bengal has to offer.

Rasogolla

রসগোল্লা

Light, spongy balls of fresh chhena simmered in sugar syrup. Created in Kolkata, beloved worldwide — a GI-tagged pride of Bengal.

Sandesh

সন্দেশ

Delicate, barely-sweet confections molded into exquisite shapes — from fish to flowers. The jewel of the Bengali misti-wala's display.

Mishti Doi

মিষ্টি দই

Creamy, caramel-tinted yogurt set in earthen pots — slightly tangy, deeply sweet. An essential finale to any Bengali meal.

Langcha

লাংচা

An elongated, deep-fried chhena sweet dipped in sugar syrup — crispy outside, meltingly soft within. Shaktigarh's legendary contribution.

Chomchom

চমচম

Oval-shaped, firm sweets coated in dried coconut, filled with sweet cream — a specialty of Porabari with roots in undivided Bengal.

Pantua

পান্তুয়া

Soft chhena and khoya dumplings fried to golden brown and soaked in cardamom-scented syrup. The Bengali ancestor of the gulab jamun.

Malpua

মালপোয়া

Crisp-edged pancakes drenched in sugar syrup, served with thickened rabri — a festive monsoon treat that fills the air with sweetness.

Patishapta

পাটিসাপটা

Delicate rice-flour crepes rolled with kheer or coconut filling — a Makar Sankranti specialty that marks winter's sweetest moments.

Nolen Gurer Sandesh

নলেন গুড়ের সন্দেশ

Winter's most prized sweet — chhena infused with date palm jaggery, smoky and deeply aromatic. Available only during the cold months.

Sitabhog

সীতাভোগ

Fine, rice-like sweet granules from Bardhaman — delicate, fragrant, and unique to Bengal. A GI-tagged heritage delicacy.

Mihidana

মিহিদানা

Tiny golden boondi pearls soaked in saffron syrup — Sitabhog's inseparable pair from Bardhaman. Together they make a royal duo.

Kheer Kadam

ক্ষীরকদম

A ball of khoya encasing a small rasgulla at its heart — the two-in-one genius of Bengali confectionery, sweet and surprising.
